Gratitude
The day everything changed
This all started on 28 December, when Fatih met Dian for the first time. I’m glad to have met you — more
than you will ever know.
I wasn’t looking. I wasn’t ready. I had walls up that I thought no one could climb. But then you showed
up — not with force, not with pressure — just with you being you. And that
was enough to change everything.

You are the best thing I never planned.
Before you, I didn’t know what it felt like to be truly seen. I walked through life carrying things
alone, convincing myself that’s just how it was supposed to be. But then you looked at me — really looked
at me — and suddenly I didn’t want to carry it alone anymore.
I thank God every single day for putting you in my path. Not because my life was empty before you — but
because you made me realize how much fuller it could be. You opened my eyes to a kind of love I didn’t
think I deserved. You showed me that being vulnerable isn’t weakness — it’s the bravest thing a man can
do.
And now that I’ve found you, the desire to have you — to keep you, to protect what we have — <span

Page Two
Love
The things I can never let go
Three months. That’s all it took for you to become the person I think about first when I wake up and last
before I fall asleep.
I love the way you care — how you ask me what I’m eating, tell me to be careful, call me back even when
you’re exhausted from work. The way you redirect your own stress just to make sure I’m okay. You carry so
much, Dian, and yet you still find room to carry me too. I see that. I will never
take that for granted.

I love the way you fight — not against me, but for us. When you got angry at
me for not focusing on the road, it wasn’t cruelty. It was fear. Fear of losing me. And knowing that your
anger comes from love? That changed how I see everything.
I miss you in ways I can’t explain. The distance between us feels like an ocean some nights. I miss
hearing your voice crack when you laugh too hard. I miss how you light up over the smallest things — a
sticker, a flower, a silly meme. I miss the nights where our 2 AM calls stretch into 3 AM because neither
of us wants to hang up first.
What I can never let go about you: the way you fold origami hearts with words that hit harder than any
poem. The way you write letters by hand in an age where no one writes anymore. The way you said <span
class="highlight">“you understand me like no one else ever has” — and how that sentence made me
want to understand you even deeper, every single day.
We built compromises that most people don’t build in years. We set boundaries. We had hard conversations
about expectations, dealbreakers, and futures. And we did it not because we had to — but because we both
wanted this to last.
You are not just someone I love.You are someone I chose to build with.
Page Three
Commitment
My oath to you
Dian, I want you to hear this — not as sweet words, but as an oath.
I will be steady, consistent, and intentional — because you taught me that a
man sets the tone of a relationship, and a woman reflects what he gives. I heard you. I will never make
you beg for reassurance. You will feel it in how I show up. Daily. Without exception.
I will never stop trying. Not a perfect man — but a man who tries. A man
who is hardworking, who will go above and beyond, who is imperfect but working towards being better. For
you. Because of you.

When you’re difficult, I stay. When you push me away because you’re scared, I stay. When the world is
getting mean to you — run to me. I will be there.
I will speak up about what hurts me, and I will listen when you speak up about what hurts you — because
that’s what real communication is. I will not cross the lines you’ve drawn. I will not make you question
whether I was ever planning to stay.
I’ll take care of me for you, if you’ll take care of you for me. That’s all I
ask.
You once wrote that I was the first person who made you feel both wildly unsure and unwaveringly certain.
I want you to know — I felt the exact same way. I had no idea what I was doing. But I knew exactly why I
had to.
Deep soul connections never go away.
If it’s real, it will never be over.
And this — us — is the most real thing I’ve ever known.
I’m not going anywhere.
I’m choosing you today, tomorrow, and every day after.
Not because you need me to.
But because you deserve someone who will —
and I refuse to let that someone be anyone else.
Forever yours, Fatih
